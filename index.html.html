How It Works

A two-stage sweepstakes running through the entire World Cup — from the Group Stage all the way to the Final. Points accumulate throughout. The highest total wins.

Stage 1 — Group Stage

Your Picks

Each player selects 2 teams before the Group Stage begins — one from the top tier, one from the bottom tier. This ensures no one gets stuck with two giants or two minnows.

Pick One

Top Tier Teams

FIFA World Ranking #1–#16 among qualified teams. The favourites, the giants. High floor, lower upside.

Pick Two

Lower Tier Teams

FIFA World Ranking #17–#48 among qualified teams. The underdogs. High risk, potentially massive upside.

Draft Order: Use a random draw to determine player pick order. Player 1 picks their Top Tier team first, then continues in reverse (snake draft) for the Lower Tier picks. This keeps things competitive and fair.

Stage 2 — Knockout Stage

Knockout Picks

Once the Group Stage ends and the Round of 32 is set, each player picks 2 additional teams for the Knockout stage — again using the tiered draft. These teams only earn points from Round of 32 onwards.

Note: You can pick a team you already hold in the Group Stage! Owning the same team in both stages lets points double-stack. But it locks you out of another pick, so weigh it carefully.

Knockout Stage

Round Bonuses

In the Knockout stage, base match points are multiplied by a round factor. The deeper your team goes, the more each point is worth.

Penalty Shootout Rule: If a knockout match goes to penalties, the winning team scores +3 pts (result points only). No goal/concede points are added for penalty goals — only the result bonus applies.

Special Powers

Super Chips

⚡ The Super Chip

Each player receives 2 Super Chips — one valid in the Group Stage, one valid in the Knockout Stage. When activated, all points (positive or negative) from that game are doubled.

Chips must be declared before kick-off of the chosen match. No late calls.

  • Chip #1 can only be used in the Group Stage (Matchdays 1–3)
  • Chip #2 can only be used in the Knockout Stage (R32 onwards)
  • Unused chips expire — they don't roll over
  • You cannot use both chips on the same team in the same game

Fresh Ideas

These are optional additions you can layer on to make the sweepstakes more fun, dramatic, and social.

🃏

The Wildcard Joker

Once per tournament (after the Group Stage), each player may swap one of their Knockout picks for any available team — paid for by spending 10 of their accumulated points. High risk, high reward.

Optional · Post-groups
🎯

Prediction Bonus

Before the tournament, each player submits a secret envelope with their predicted tournament winner. If correct: +15 points. Runners-up prediction: +7 points. Semi-finalist: +3 points.

Optional · Pre-tournament
💸

Mid-Tournament Transfer Window

After the Group Stage, open a one-hour "transfer window" in a group call. Players who finished bottom-half of the table at group stage can trade one of their knocked-out teams with another player for a fee of 5 points.

Optional · Fun & social
😈

Curse Card

Each player gets one Curse Card. Use it to give any opponent −5 points. But beware — you can only use it once, and others can target you. Best used on the leader. Creates chaos and drama.

Optional · Chaotic
📊

Live Leaderboard

Maintain a WhatsApp-pinned or Google Sheets leaderboard that updates after every matchday. Seeing the standings in real-time keeps people engaged even when their teams aren't playing.

Recommended · Always-on
🏅

Side Wagers

During knockouts, players can issue head-to-head wagers: "My Brazil beats your Germany to the quarters — loser buys the winner a beer." These are informal and purely for bragging rights.

Admin Guide

Organiser Checklist

2–3 Weeks Before
Confirm Participants & Entry Fee

Lock in your ~10 players. Agree on the entry fee and prize structure. Collect fees upfront via UPI/cash.

1 Week Before
Seed the Teams by FIFA Ranking

Pull the latest FIFA World Rankings and filter to the 48 qualified teams. Split into Top 16 and Bottom 32. Share the seeded list with players.

2–3 Days Before
Draft Night (Group Stage Picks)

Run the snake draft for Group Stage picks. Randomise order, pick Top Tier first, then reverse for Lower Tier. Collect prediction envelopes if using that option.

During Group Stage
Track Points & Chip Declarations

Collect Super Chip declarations before each game via the group chat. Update leaderboard after every matchday.

After Group Stage
Knockout Draft + Transfer Window

Run the Knockout picks draft. If using the Transfer Window feature, host a quick group call. Activate any Wildcards.

Final Weekend
Final Scores & Prize Distribution

Calculate final totals. Open the prediction envelopes. Announce the winner and distribute prizes with appropriate theatre.

Quick Reference

The Rules,
Plain & Simple

Each player picks 2 teams for the Group Stage — one from FIFA Top 16, one from the rest of the qualified field — via a snake draft.

After the Group Stage, each player picks 2 more teams for the Knockout Stage. You may re-pick a team you already hold.

Scoring: +3 Win · +1 Draw · +1 per goal scored · −1 per goal conceded · +2 Clean Sheet bonus.

Knockout stage points are multiplied by the round: R32 ×1 · R16 ×1.5 · QF ×2 · SF ×3 · Final ×5.

Penalty shootouts: only the +3 win result applies. No goal/concede points counted for penalty kicks.

Each player has 2 Super Chips (one per stage). Declare before kick-off to double all points for that game — positive or negative.

All picks, chip declarations, and optional power plays must be submitted before the relevant kick-off. Late submissions are void.

The organiser's ruling is final on any disputes. Keep it fun.
